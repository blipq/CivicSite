The Youth and
Social Media
The Issue

It’s safe to say that the internet has opened many doors in the realm of innovation across the globe, but that level of confidence cannot be shared with the well-being of children who have access to the world-wide web. As social media continues to grow in popularity, so too does the risk of harm and exposure to inappropriate content on these platforms. With most of these services allowing users of all ages to join, the safety of children utilizing them is seldom guaranteed. While many media sites limit access to harmful material, none of them can protect their users from other users. The risk of being socially attacked or even hacked is far too great. Children may unknowingly share private information about their system and accounts, or share too much about themselves or their family, giving bad actors all they need to execute their malicious intent. Even if a child was perfectly safe on the internet, their presence may only exist to get away from a poor relationship with their family, or to give said family a moment of silence, as a form of pacification, ultimately neglecting their responsibilities as parents or guardians. As it is impossible to monitor even just the five percent of the open-web we use on a daily basis, no one can guarantee the safety of children from inappropriate and explicit material. Social media today has moderate protection against this content, but no site is able to completely eliminate it. With pop culture being highly discussed on social media, and much of it intended for more mature audiences, children will find it challenging to avoid it; in fact, they will likely be drawn to it, as it is considered popular and important. Additionally, with many children having unrestricted access to the internet, this material is often shared between children. According to Internetmatters.org, roughly 1 in 4 children online have been sent explicit content by their peers, and over 9 in 10 children have been exposed to this content by the age of 14. These numbers make it clear that social media has a very unfortunate grip on the new generation. Not all media sites are negative, or at least unnecessary for children and young adults. Some useful ones include educational forum pages, like Quizlet, Khan Academy, Quora, and Stack Overflow. Asking questions and seeking help in these social platforms are generally accompanied with positive assistance and feedback, but it isn’t unlikely for someone to respond harshly, and act deprecative. This kind of behavior is even more common on less regulated sites, such as major social media outlets. Users can harass, attack, and even belittle others, especially children, as there are little to no repercussions to their actions. This is because the internet grants individuals a sense of anonymity, making children easy targets to this kind of behavior. With social media constantly growing, and with almost no connections to an individual’s physical presence, it’s the perfect place for cyberbullying. The influence of social media on today's youth is undeniable, bringing in an era of unprecedented connectivity but also introducing quite a few security and safety concerns. As the digital landscape continues to evolve, the youth find themselves navigating a complex terrain, where personal information is often shared without a full understanding of the potential consequences. The constant connection shared can overshadow the risks associated with online platforms, exposing the youth to issues like cyberbullying, identity theft, and privacy breaches. The boundary between the virtual and real worlds becomes blurred, raising questions about the long-term impact on mental health and self-esteem. As social media platforms become breeding grounds for misinformation and predators, addressing these security and safety challenges is of utmost importance. Finding a balance between fostering a sense of community and ensuring the protection of the youth requires a concerted effort from both platform developers and parents alike. Only through an all encompassing approach, that includes education on online safety, robust privacy settings, and vigilant monitoring, can we hope to change the effects of social media on the welfare of our youth Excessive use of technology has been associated with multiple detrimental effects on children. Prolonged screen time can produce “Mindless eating,” where a child is unaware of how much they are consuming because they are focused on their screen. Additionally, the attention span of children is greatly reduced due to the readiness of media because of advances in technology. As outlined in an article by National University, a research study in Canada revealed that the average attention span dropped from roughly 12 seconds to only eight seconds. Academic performance may also suffer due to this loss of attention span, as children are less likely to focus on their studies. While acknowledging the benefits of technology, parents must establish a healthy balance and employ measures to help relieve the negative impacts on their children's health and well-being. In its infancy, social media had much fewer users who regulated their time off the internet much more successfully than today’s users. Today, 97 percent of teenagers use at least one major social media outlet, and 47 percent are online almost constantly, according to Mayoclinic.org. The average workday for a student in the teenage age range is about eight hours including transportation to and from school while the recommended sleep for a student of this age is eight to ten hours each night. This allots only a few hours for any teenager to use social media; however, any student spending more than six to eight hours on social media is sacrificing time that should be put to learning or sleeping. This addiction negatively impacts the lives of the teenagers by diminishing their grades as a result of them dedicating more time to social media rather than academics, and physically affects them due to the teenagers dedicating time to social media that they should be using to rest.
